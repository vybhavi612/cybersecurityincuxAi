Phishing Prevention Tips

Stay ahead of attackers with these proven security practices.

01

Verify the Domain

Always check the root domain, not just the subdomain. paypal.secure-verify.com is owned by secure-verify.com, not PayPal.

02

Check for HTTPS

Legitimate sites use HTTPS. However, HTTPS alone doesn't guarantee safety — phishing sites can also use SSL certificates.

03

Expand Shortened URLs

Before clicking a bit.ly or t.co link, preview the destination using a URL expander service such as checkshorturl.com.

04

Look for Lookalike Domains

Attackers use domains like paypa1.com (with a 1 instead of l) or аmаzon.com using Cyrillic characters.

05

Never Enter Credentials via Email Links

Navigate directly to websites by typing the address manually. Never click login links in unsolicited emails or messages.

06

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication

MFA prevents attackers from accessing your account even if they obtain your password through a phishing attack.

07

Use a Password Manager

Password managers only auto-fill credentials on the correct domain, giving you a built-in phishing protection layer.

08

Keep Software Updated

Browser and OS updates include security patches that protect against known phishing techniques and malware delivery methods.

09

Report Phishing Attempts

Report phishing emails to reportphishing@apwg.org and suspicious URLs to Google Safe Browsing.
